You could bathe in milk, wine, or chicken boullion, so thank you for choosing us instead.

Shinrin yoku (森林浴) lit. "forest bathing" is a term popularized by the Japanese government in 1982, describing the practice of walking in the woods for health benefits. Since then, there's been considerable research published on the topic, like phytoncides, for instance. Of course, we recommend you try the real thing.

This blend is for the city folk and desert dwellers that could benefit from bringing a bit of the forest into their baths.
